PORTFOLIO

Theme 12: Design for Developers

To break down this theme, we worked on four major topics, and throughout these two weeks we gathered a good size toolbox to master a good visual presentation. First out we larned how to work with and apply fonts and colors, and had a look at best practices for the portfolio format. In addition to master the art of font pairing and colorpalettes, we learned about conventions for UI-cards, responsive design and different variations of animations. We also touched upon frameworks.

FONTS AND CARDS

Key words:

Serif, sans serif, display fonts, font pairing, font hierarchy, line height, base line, conventions, portfolio vs. landingpages, white space, alignment, repetition.

In conclusion

This theme has been a pleasure, I enjoyed the creative processes and learning new tools I can now add to my toolbox, and cool features I can implement in my proffesional portfolio. There are some of the projects where I wish I had some more time to fully understand all of the consepts, but with a busy schedule, you sometimes have to accept a result that is not completely perfect.

For another time, I would really like to implement GSAP scrolltriggers on my cards, and subtile animations in general to extend the presentation of my knowledge. I actually found a few examples that had all of the features I would like to have on my scrolltrigger, but I did not succeed on implementing it 100% the way I wanted to - and therefor chose to not go further on with that. In addition, I think it would really add something interesting to the design visually and technically, to add som animated SVG's. But that will have to be another time!
