FONTS AND CARDS

Font hierarchy and UI-card conventions

This assignment consisted of two parts. Part one was to create a font hierarchy with one or two fonts. Following the convensions, I chose a display font for headings and a sans serif for bodytext and CTAs. Even though most screens these days have a high quality resolution, it is often best to go with a sans serif for this, simply beacuse this is more readable comared to a serif - and being readable is the only task a bodytext an CTAs have.

In part two we were given a template with UI-cards, but without any styling and they were not following the conventions and best practices we had seen plenty of examples of. Our job here was to impprove the UI-cards following the guidelines for designing UI cards, in addition to create a better looking card using CSS techniques such as grid and flex.
